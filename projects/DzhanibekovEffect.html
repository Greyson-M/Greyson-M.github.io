Dzhanibekov Effect

The Dzhanibekov effect is a phenomenon in which a rotating rigid body exhibits unexpected motion due to its moments of inertia.

The effect is a result of the intermediate axis theorem, which states that a rigid body rotating about its intermediate axis is unstable.

This effect comes from Euler's equations of motion for a rigid body:

$$I_1 \dot{\omega}_1 = (I_2 - I_3) \omega_2 \omega_3$$

$$I_2 \dot{\omega}_2 = (I_3 - I_1) \omega_3 \omega_1$$

$$I_3 \dot{\omega}_3 = (I_1 - I_2) \omega_1 \omega_2$$
